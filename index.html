Dr. Stephen Hawking

A timeline of Dr. Stephen Hawking's life.

  • January 8th, 1942 - Born in Oxford, England, United Kingdom.
  • 1950 - Hawking's father becomes head of the divison of parasitology at the National Institute for Medical Research, causing Hawking and his family to move to St. Albans Hertfordshire.
  • 1952 - Hawking begins attending St. Albans School.
  • 1958 - Hawking with help from his mathematics teacher Dikran Tahta builds a bomputer with recycled parts.
  • 1958 - Hawking begins studying Physics and Chemistry against his father and headmaster's advice.
  • 1959 - Hawking begins attending Oxford.
  • 1962 - Hawking receives a first-class BA(Hons.) degree in physics, and begins his graduate work at Cambridge.
  • 1962 - Hawking is diagnosed with motor neurone disease, and develops depression.
  • 1966 - Hawkin's Thesis, "Properties of expanding universes" is approved.
  • 1966 - Hawkin's essay, "Singularities and the Geometry of Space-Time" shares top honours for the Adams Prize.
  • 1970 - Hawking and his colleague Penrose prove that if the Universe obeys the general theory of relativity and any of Alexander Friedmann's moedls of physical cosmology, then the Universe must have started as a singularity.
  • 1970 - Hawking postulates the second law of black hole dynamics.
  • 1973 - Hawking begins to study quantum gravity and quantum mechanics.
  • 1974 - Hawking discovers Hawking radiation
  • 1974 - Hawking is elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1975 - Hawking returns to Cambridge as a reader in gravitational physics
  • 1975 - Hawking is awarded the Eddington Medal and the Pius XI Gold Medal.
  • 1976 Hawking wins the Dannie Heineman Prize, the Maxwell Prize, and theHughes Medal.
  • 1978 - Hawking receives the Albert Einstein Medal and an honorary doctorate from the University of Oxford.
  • 1979 - Hawking is elected Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge.
  • 1981 - At a Vatican confrence hawking presents work suggesting there is no beginning or end of the universe.
  • 1983 - Hawking withh his colleague Jim Hartle propose that prior to the Planck epoch time did not exist.
  • 1984 - Hawking publishes, "A Brief History of Time."
  • 1985 - Hawking is awarded the Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society.
  • 1987 - Hawking is awarded the Paul Dirac Medal.
  • 1988 - Hawking and Penrose win the Wolf Prize.
  • 2001 - Hawking publishes "The Universe in a Nutshell."
  • 2005 - Hawking publishes "A Briefer History of Time."
  • 2006 - Hawking publishes "God Created the Integers"
  • 2015 - Hawking states that not all information is lost when something enters a black hole.
  • March 14th, 2018 - Hawking dies of Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.
  • April 2018 - Hawkin's final paper, "A smooth exit from eternal inflation?" is published.
